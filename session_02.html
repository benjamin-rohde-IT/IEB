Information Economics & Business

Session 2:
Network Effects & Digital Markets

Understanding how network effects, platforms, and digital ecosystems create concentrated markets, lock-in effects, and winner-take-all dynamics.

Core Exam Theme

Why digital markets often become dominated by a few powerful platform firms.

What This Session Is Really About

Session 2 focuses on the economics of digital markets. Unlike traditional industries, many digital businesses become more valuable as more users join the platform.

This creates network effects: each additional user increases value for existing users. Strong network effects can produce highly concentrated markets, platform dominance, and winner-take-all outcomes.

The session also examines why firms increasingly compete through ecosystems rather than isolated products. Platforms such as OpenAI, Google, Amazon, and Apple coordinate entire ecosystems of developers, users, complementors, and data infrastructures.

Important Strategic Insight

In digital markets, competitive advantage often comes from ecosystem control, user lock-in, data accumulation, and platform coordination — not merely superior products.

Core Concepts

Network Effects

The value of a product or platform increases as more users join.

Winner-Take-All Markets

Markets where one or a few firms dominate because of scale and network effects.

Platform Ecosystems

Firms create value by coordinating interactions between multiple participant groups.

Lock-In Effects

Users become dependent on a platform because switching becomes costly.

How This Connects to Your Synopsis

OpenAI as a Platform Actor

OpenAI increasingly functions as a platform ecosystem. More users generate more data and feedback, which improves models, which attracts more users and partners.

Strategic Dependency Risks

Novo Nordisk may gain significant AI capabilities through OpenAI, but the partnership also raises questions about dependence, bargaining power, and long-term control over strategic infrastructure.

Likely Oral Exam Questions

Does OpenAI benefit from network effects?

How you should answer

Explain that more users generate more prompts, feedback, and usage data. This improves model quality and strengthens ecosystem attractiveness. Then connect this to competitive advantage and scale.

Why do digital markets often become concentrated?

How you should answer

Discuss network effects, economies of scale, data accumulation, switching costs, and ecosystem coordination. Explain why these mechanisms reinforce dominant firms over time.

Could pharmaceutical firms become dependent on AI platforms?

How you should answer

Yes — especially if core AI infrastructure, models, and data capabilities are externally controlled. Discuss lock-in, bargaining power, strategic dependence, and reduced organizational autonomy.

Important Examiner Advice

The examiner will likely test whether you understand the mechanisms behind digital dominance. Do not merely define network effects. Explain HOW they shape competition, WHY they create concentrated markets, and WHAT strategic consequences they create for firms like Novo Nordisk.
