Theory 01

Transaction Cost Theory (TCT)

Based on Williamson (1979, 1986), TCT posits that the optimal organizational structure is one that achieves economic efficiency by minimizing the sum of Production Costs and Transaction Costs.

🔎

Search Costs

Costs of finding a suitable partner or supplier in the market (Medaglia, Session 8).

📝

Information Costs

Costs of measuring and identifying the quality of a product or service before transacting.

🤝

Bargaining Costs

Costs of negotiating and reaching an acceptable agreement with another party.

👮

Policing Costs

Costs of monitoring and enforcing the terms of the contract to prevent opportunism.

Theory 02

Digitalization and Transaction Costs

Malone et al. (1987) and Medaglia (Session 8) argue that digital tools significantly reduce coordination costs, leading to three major shifts in organizational boundaries.

Theory 03

Transaction Risks in Digital Economies

While digitalization reduces many costs, it introduces new risks (Medaglia, Session 8; Nagle et al., 2025). Organizations must balance efficiency against these potential downsides.

😈

Opportunism

The risk of a partner acting in their own self-interest at your expense. Highly relevant in "Small Numbers Bargaining" (limited suppliers).

💤

Shirking

Operation risk where the partner under-performs because their outputs are hard to monitor digitally (Medaglia, Session 8).

🔒

Asset Specificity

When an investment (data, custom API) is only valuable within one specific partnership. This leads to Lock-in.

Case Application

Novo Nordisk — The Outsourcing Logic

Analyzing Novo Nordisk's strategic choices through TCT:

  • Production vs. Transaction Costs: Building a world-class LLM internally would have prohibitive production costs for Novo. Partnering with OpenAI allows them to access lower production costs via a market transaction.
  • Search Costs: Digitalization (and OpenAI's fame) reduced the search costs for an AI partner to near-zero.
  • Small Numbers Risk: There are only a few players who can offer what OpenAI does. This creates a "Small Numbers" bargaining situation, increasing the risk of Opportunism or Lock-in for Novo Nordisk.

Exam Preparation

Likely Oral Exam Questions

  • Core How does TCT explain why firms exist in the first place? ▶
    • Firms exist when the cost of coordinating through the **Market** (transaction costs) is higher than the cost of coordinating within a **Hierarchy** (internal management costs).
    • If markets were perfect and costless, every task would be outsourced to the lowest bidder.
  • Core What is the "Electronic Market Hypothesis" (Malone et al., 1987)? ▶
    • Hypothesis that IT reduces coordination costs more than production costs.
    • Because market coordination is generally more efficient at producing goods, a drop in coordination costs will favor **Markets** over Hierarchies.
    • This leads to more outsourcing and more fluid organizational boundaries.
  • Synthesis Can digitalization actually INCREASE transaction costs? Explain with an example. ▶
    • Yes. Specifically **Policing/Monitoring costs**.
    • In complex AI partnerships, the "Black Box" nature makes it hard to verify if the partner is performing optimally (Hidden Action).
    • Asymmetric information in digital contracts can lead to higher monitoring costs to prevent **Shirking**.
