Information Economics & Business

Session 1:
Digitalization, AI & Data

Understanding how AI, digitalization, and data reshape competitive advantage, firm strategy, organizational capabilities, and market structures.

Core Exam Theme

How digital technologies fundamentally change competition and strategic management.

What This Session Is Really About

Session 1 establishes the intellectual foundation for the course. The core argument is that digitalization is not simply about adopting technology. It fundamentally changes how firms create value, compete, learn, and organize.

AI and data-intensive technologies create new forms of competitive advantage. Firms increasingly compete based on prediction capabilities, data access, automation, algorithmic learning, and ecosystem positioning.

Traditional competitive advantages such as scale economies, physical assets, or operational efficiency are increasingly complemented — and sometimes replaced — by data accumulation, machine learning feedback loops, and digital infrastructures.

Important Strategic Insight

Digitalization changes the economics of information. Information becomes cheaper to collect, process, store, and analyze. This enables firms to coordinate activities differently, improve decision-making, personalize offerings, and automate cognitive work.

Core Concepts

Digitalization

Transformation of business processes, markets, and organizational structures through digital technologies.

AI as Prediction

AI lowers the cost of prediction and enables automation of decision-making processes.

Data Network Effects

More users generate more data, which improves algorithms and creates stronger products.

Digital Ecosystems

Firms increasingly compete within interconnected platform ecosystems.

How This Connects to Your Synopsis

Novo Nordisk × OpenAI

Your synopsis directly fits the logic introduced in Session 1. Novo Nordisk is responding to the increasing strategic importance of AI, data, and digital capabilities in pharmaceutical innovation.

Strong Analytical Angle

You should argue that AI is not merely an operational tool. It changes organizational learning, R&D productivity, strategic flexibility, and long-term competitive positioning.

Likely Oral Exam Questions

Why is AI strategically important for incumbent firms?

How you should answer

Explain that AI improves prediction capabilities, accelerates decision-making, enables automation, and enhances organizational learning. Then connect this to how incumbents risk losing competitiveness if they fail to adapt.

How does data become a source of competitive advantage?

How you should answer

Mention feedback loops: more users create more data, which improves algorithms, which improves products, which attracts even more users. Also discuss proprietary datasets and learning advantages.

How is digital transformation different from traditional IT adoption?

How you should answer

Traditional IT primarily improves efficiency. Digital transformation changes business models, organizational structures, capabilities, and strategic positioning. Emphasize that digitalization is organizational and strategic — not only technological.

Important Examiner Advice

The examiner will rarely reward purely descriptive answers. You need to demonstrate analytical understanding. Always explain WHY the theory matters strategically, HOW it changes firm behavior, and HOW it connects to your specific case.
