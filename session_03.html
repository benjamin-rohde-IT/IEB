Information Economics & Business

Session 3:
Pricing & Revenue Models

Understanding how digital firms monetize products and services through pricing strategies, versioning, bundling, price discrimination, and platform-based business models.

Core Exam Theme

How firms design pricing and monetization strategies to capture value in digital markets.

What This Session Is Really About

Session 3 addresses a fundamental challenge for digital firms: how to monetize products and services when marginal costs approach zero. Traditional pricing strategies based on cost-plus markup fail in this environment.

Digital firms must design sophisticated pricing and revenue models. These include versioning strategies (offering multiple quality tiers), bundling (combining products to increase value capture), price discrimination (charging different prices to different customer segments), and freemium models (free basic service + premium paid features).

The session also explores how platform business models differ from traditional pricing. Platforms often use pricing to balance multiple participant groups: subsidizing one side to attract participants, while monetizing through another side.

Important Strategic Insight

In digital markets, pricing is not just about covering costs and making profit. It is a strategic tool for market positioning, customer segmentation, value extraction, and managing competitive dynamics. Poor pricing can destroy value even in a successful business.

Core Concepts

Versioning

Offering multiple product versions (basic, premium, enterprise) to capture value from different customer segments.

Bundling

Combining multiple products or services into a single offering to increase willingness to pay.

Price Discrimination

Charging different customers different prices based on their willingness to pay or use patterns.

Freemium Models

Providing free base service to attract users, then monetizing premium features or power users.

How This Connects to Your Synopsis

OpenAI's Monetization Strategy

OpenAI employs multiple revenue models: ChatGPT Plus (subscription for power users), API access (pay-per-token for developers), and enterprise licensing. This is sophisticated price discrimination and versioning.

Novo Nordisk's Strategic Advantage

By partnering with OpenAI, Novo gains access to AI capabilities without the burden of monetization strategy development. This allows Novo to integrate AI into pharmaceutical R&D and potential customer offerings.

Likely Oral Exam Questions

How do AI firms monetize their products?

How you should answer

Explain multiple strategies: subscription models (ChatGPT Plus), API pricing (pay-per-use), enterprise licensing, freemium approaches (free access with limits). Discuss why each strategy targets different customer segments.

Why do AI firms use freemium models?

How you should answer

Freemium models serve multiple purposes: attracting users and building network effects, generating data to improve products, creating a funnel to convert power users to paid tiers, maintaining competitive positioning against free alternatives.

What role does bundling play in digital markets?

How you should answer

Bundling increases customer willingness to pay by creating the perception of higher value. It also improves customer lock-in and reduces churn. Bundles can segment customers: charging premium prices to customers with high valuations while still capturing price-sensitive users.

Important Examiner Advice

The examiner will test whether you understand pricing as a strategic tool, not just a revenue mechanism. Avoid simplistic answers about "profit maximization." Instead, explain HOW pricing strategies align with competitive positioning, market structure, and customer value creation in your specific case analysis.
